Indian Astronomical Instruments

Explore the science and history behind India's ancient astronomical tools.

Jantar Mantar

An ancient observatory with massive instruments for celestial tracking and timekeeping.

Astrolabe

A portable device used to measure stars’ positions and solve astronomical problems.

Sundial

Uses the sun’s shadow to indicate the time of day, one of the oldest timekeeping tools.

Samrat Yantra

A giant sundial at Jantar Mantar used for calculating local time with great precision.

Jaiprakash Yantra

Consists of hemispherical bowls for observing the position of celestial objects.

Rashivalaya Yantra

Dedicated to the twelve zodiac signs for accurate astrological measurements.

Shankh Yantra

A symbolic conch-shaped instrument used in rituals and aligned with cosmic energies.

Dhanur Yantra

Used to measure angles of elevation and trajectories, inspired by archery geometry.

Kundali Yantra

An astrological chart device for mapping planetary positions and horoscopes.
